Day-by-Day Itinerary

Ayrshire coast to St. Andrews — June 14–21, 2026. Organized by Hidden Links Golf.

Day 2 Royal Troon ⛳ Mon, Jun 15

The Postage Stamp awaits. Royal Troon has hosted The Open 11 times — today it hosts your group. The 8th hole is just 123 yards but don’t let that fool you.

  • 1:30 PM Transfer Depart Turnberry for Royal Troon (Craig Bilham Executive Travel)
  • 3:20 PM Golf Royal Troon — tee time 3:20 × 8. Old Course, Par 71, 7,175 yards. Home of the Postage Stamp (8th, 123 yards) and The Railway (11th). Championship pedigree since 1878.
  • ~8:30 PM Transfer Return pick-up, back to Turnberry
  • Overnight Hotel Turnberry, a Luxury Collection Resort — same rooms as Day 1. Breakfast included.
Day 3 Turnberry Ailsa Course → St. Andrews ⛳ Tue, Jun 16

Wake up at Turnberry, play the course right outside your door, then pack up and head east to St. Andrews for the rest of the trip. Morning rounds under the Ailsa Craig view before the big transfer.

  • ~4:15 PM Transfer Depart Turnberry for St. Andrews (~2.5 hrs, Craig Bilham Executive Travel)
  • Overnight Hotel Greyfriars Hotel — 129 North Street, St. Andrews (arrival June 16, through June 21). Breakfast included.
  • Overnight Hotel Lorimer House — 19 Murray Park, St. Andrews. Self check-in code: 8591 on front door keypad. Keys & room assignment in the dining room. Check-in 3pm / Check-out 10am. Breakfast included.
  • Overnight Hotel Nethan Guest House — 17 Murray Park, St. Andrews. Family twin rooms. Breakfast included.
📌 For Lorimer House: if arriving outside normal hours, use door code 8591. Your room number is posted in the dining room. Questions: call Scott on +44 (0)7841 669544.
Day 4 Golf House Club, Elie ⛳ Wed, Jun 17

Head down the Fife coast to Elie — one of Scotland’s most historic and intimate links. Golf has been played here since the 15th century. Ask the starter about the periscope.

  • 10:30 AM Transfer Depart St. Andrews for Elie (Club Cars)
  • 12:00 PM Golf Golf House Club, Elie — tee time 12:00 × 8. Old Tom Morris design (1895). 16 par 4s, 2 par 3s, 6,273 yards. Famous for 8 front-to-back sloped greens and the dramatic blind opening drive over a hill.
  • Must See 🔬 The starter’s hut houses a Royal Navy periscope from HMS Excalibur — used to check the 1st fairway before play. A genuine naval artifact on a golf course.
  • ~5:15 PM Transfer Return to St. Andrews (Club Cars)
  • Overnight Hotel Greyfriars / Lorimer House / Nethan (same as Day 3)
Day 5 St. Andrews Jubilee Course ⛳ Thu, Jun 18

No transport today — the course is a short walk from your digs. The Jubilee is the toughest of the St. Andrews seven, redesigned to championship standard in 1988. Coastal wind, exposed greens, and views across St. Andrews Bay.

  • 2:32 PM Golf St. Andrews Jubilee Course — tee time 2:32 × 8. ~6,800 yards. Opened 1897 for Queen Victoria’s Diamond Jubilee, completely redesigned by Donald Steel in 1988. Considered the most underrated of St. Andrews’ seven courses.
  • Walk On Foot No transportation today — walk to and from the course from your accommodation
  • Overnight Hotel Greyfriars / Lorimer House / Nethan
⛳ The Jubilee sits wedged between the sea and the New Course. Elevated tees give unparalleled views across St Andrews Bay. No shared greens or fairways — every hole stands on its own.
Day 6 Old Course Ballot & Lundin Golf Club ⛳ Fri, Jun 19

Two-round day if the ballot gods smile. If the Old Course comes through, take that walk in the morning, then head south to Lundin in the afternoon. Lundin alone would be a highlight on any other trip.

  • Morning Golf Old Course at St. Andrews (ballot) — if successful in the daily ballot, walk to and from the course. The ballot draw happens the evening before. Par 72, 7,305 yards, Swilcan Bridge, Hell Bunker, Road Hole. Old Course must be paid directly on the day.
  • 10:45 AM Transfer Depart St. Andrews for Lundin Golf Club (Club Cars)
  • 12:20 PM Golf Lundin Golf Club — tee time 12:20 × 8. Founded 1868, designed by Old Tom Morris and James Braid. Par 75, 6,371 yards. Former Open Championship qualifying venue. Pre-historic Standing Stones of Lundin on the adjacent ladies’ course date to 2000 BC.
  • ~5:30 PM Transfer Return to St. Andrews (Club Cars)
  • Overnight Hotel Greyfriars / Lorimer House / Nethan
🎲 Old Course ballot is not guaranteed. If it doesn’t come through, the group can still walk the course freely, cross the Swilcan Bridge, and get every photo you want — the course is public right-of-way on Sundays and when not in play.

The Courses

Eight rounds across some of the most storied links in the world — from Ayrshire to the Kingdom of Fife.

Round 1 — Jun 14
Prestwick Golf Club
📍 Prestwick, Ayrshire • Tee time: 3:36 PM

The birthplace of The Open Championship. The first Open was contested here in 1860 — eight players, 36 holes, a red Morocco belt as the prize. Old Tom Morris designed the original 12-hole course with little more than a wheelbarrow and a shovel. Six of those original greens are still in use today. Famous for blind shots over towering dunes and cavernous bunkers with no sight of the green.

⛴ Classic links • Founded 1851
🏆 Hosted the first 12 Open Championships consecutively (1860–1872)
🌟 Notable holes: 1st (Railway), 3rd (Cardinal), 5th (Himalayas), 17th (Alps)
Round 2 — Jun 15
Royal Troon
📍 Troon, Ayrshire • Tee time: 3:20 PM

Established 1878. Eleven Open Championships. The iconic Postage Stamp (8th) — 123 yards, a tiny green, and a history of wrecked scorecards. The Railway (11th) runs parallel to an actual railway line. The 6th hole is the longest par 5 in Open history at 623 yards. Royal charter granted 1978. Par 71, 7,175 yards, shaped by Willie Fernie, James Braid, and Alister MacKenzie.

⛴ Par 71 • 7,175 yards
🏆 11 Open Championships, most recent 2024
🌟 Postage Stamp (8th): 123 yards — most famous short par 3 in golf
Round 3 — Jun 16
Turnberry Ailsa Course
📍 Turnberry, Ayrshire • Tee time: 10:31 AM (Resident)

Four Open Championships and one of the most dramatic coastlines in golf. Holes 4–11 hug the Firth of Clyde with sweeping views to Ailsa Craig and the Isle of Arran. The 9th (Bruce’s Castle) plays from a rocky headland directly toward the Turnberry Lighthouse — sheer drop to the rocks below the green. Redesigned in 2016 by Martin Ebert, with further enhancements in 2025. Venue of the legendary 1977 “Duel in the Sun.”

⛴ Links • 4 Open Championships
🏆 1977 “Duel in the Sun” — Nicklaus vs. Watson
🌟 Hole 9 (Bruce’s Castle): lighthouse par 3 — sea directly behind the green
Round 4 — Jun 17
Golf House Club, Elie
📍 Elie, Fife • Tee time: 12:00 PM

Golf has been played on the Elie links since the 15th century. The current Old Tom Morris layout (1895) is unchanged — making it one of the purest links experiences in Scotland. Famous for 8 front-to-back sloped greens, 16 par 4s and 2 par 3s, four dramatic sea views, and the oldest clubhouse in active use (1875). In 2025 it was officially recognized as the third oldest golf course in the world. And yes — there’s a real submarine periscope in the starter’s hut.

⛴ Par 70 • 6,273 yards • Old Tom Morris (1895)
📅 3rd oldest golf course in the world (official, 2025)
🔬 Starter’s hut: working Royal Navy periscope (HMS Excalibur)
Round 5 — Jun 18
St. Andrews Jubilee Course
📍 St. Andrews, Fife • Tee time: 2:32 PM

Opened in 1897 for Queen Victoria’s Diamond Jubilee and completely redesigned to championship standard by Donald Steel in 1988 — making it the toughest of the seven St. Andrews courses. Wedged between St. Andrews Bay and the New Course, with no shared greens or fairways. Elevated tees give views of the North Sea and West Sands Beach. No double greens, but the same classic out-and-back routing as the Old Course. The most underrated course in St. Andrews — by a wide margin.

⛴ ~6,800 yards • Championship redesign 1988
🏆 Toughest of the 7 St. Andrews Links Trust courses
🌟 Walk from your hotel — no transport needed
Round 6 — Jun 19
Lundin Golf Club
📍 Lundin Links, Fife • Tee time: 12:20 PM

Founded 1868, designed by Old Tom Morris and later upgraded by James Braid. A former Open Championship qualifying venue along the shores of Largo Bay in Fife. Par 75, 6,371 yards. The 14th hole (“Perfection”) is a downhill par 3 with panoramic views of the Firth of Forth — from the 14th tee you can see every other tee and green on the course. Adjacent to the oldest women’s golf course in the world (1890), with prehistoric Standing Stones on the second hole dating to 2000 BC.

⛴ Par 75 • 6,371 yards • Founded 1868
🏆 Former Open Championship Final Qualifying venue
🌟 14th (Perfection): panoramic Firth of Forth par 3

Photo: Richard Webb via Wikimedia Commons (CC-BY-SA 2.0)

Round 7 — Jun 20
Dumbarnie Links
📍 Upper Largo, Fife • Tee time: 9:40 AM

The newest course on the tour — opened spring 2020 on the 5,000-acre Balcarres Estate and immediately earned its place among Scotland’s best. Designed by Clive Clark with wide fairways, elevated tees, and coastal holes along Largo Bay that rivals Pebble Beach in scale and drama. Three driveable par 4s add variety. The 10th hole was redesigned in 2025 based on player feedback. Host of the 2025 Women’s Scottish Open.

⛴ Opened 2020 • Clive Clark design
🏆 “Top Emerging Golf Course” UK • 2025 Women’s Scottish Open host
🌟 Holes 4, 9, 10, 13, 14 set along Largo Bay
Round 8 — Ballot (Jun 19, if drawn)
The Old Course at St Andrews
📍 St. Andrews, Fife — Operated by St. Andrews Links Trust

Golf has been played here since the early 15th century, making it the oldest course on earth. Originally 22 holes, standardized to 18 in 1764 — the format the whole world now uses. Seven iconic double greens. The 17th (Road Hole) is the most feared par 4 in championship golf. Hell Bunker covers 300 square yards. The Swilcan Bridge is 700 years old. Bobby Jones. Jack Nicklaus. Tiger Woods. Thirty Open Championships and counting. This is the one.

📅 Golf played here since the early 1400s • Par 72 • 7,305 yards
🏆 30 Open Championships — more than any other course on earth
🏞 7 double greens • 110 named bunkers • Swilcan Bridge (700 yrs old) • Hell Bunker: 300 sq yards
🎲 The ballot draw happens the evening before — enter the daily ballot via the St. Andrews Links Trust portal. Old Course green fees are paid directly on the day of play (not prepaid with the package). Even without a tee time, the course is open for walking on Sundays — cross the Swilcan Bridge and stand in the Valley of Sin.
