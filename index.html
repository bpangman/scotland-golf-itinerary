The Courses

Four legendary layouts across the Kingdom of Fife — plus a shot at the Old Course.

Course 1
Crail Golfing Society
📍 Crail, Fife — Balcomie Links

The 7th oldest golf club in the world, Balcomie Links is a jaw-dropping Old Tom Morris design carved into the Fife Ness coastline. Six par 3s, including a 210-yarder played over a vertical cliff. Pure links drama.

⛴ Par 69 • 5,861 yards
📅 Designed by Tom Morris, 1895
🏆 Featured in “Golf in the Kingdom”

Photo: Bursting74 via Wikimedia Commons (GFDL)

Course 2
Golf House Club, Elie
📍 Elie & Earlsferry, Fife

Golf has been played at Earlsferry Links since the 1500s. The current layout — largely unchanged since 1895 — features 16 par 4s and 2 par 3s with fast, undulating greens and sweeping seaside views. Birthplace of legends James Braid and three other Open champions.

⛴ Par 70 • 18 holes
🔬 Starter's hut has a working submarine periscope
📅 Golf played here since the 1500s

Photo: Geograph.org.uk via Wikimedia Commons (CC-BY-SA 2.0)

Course 3
Lundin Golf Club
📍 Lundin Links, Fife — 20 min from St. Andrews

A hidden gem designed by two of history’s greatest: Old Tom Morris and James Braid. Former Open Championship qualifying venue with arguably the best greens in the entire Kingdom of Fife. Complex layout with open burns, a railway boundary, and strategic bunkering.

📅 Founded May 8, 1868
⛶ Old Tom Morris & James Braid design
🏆 Former Open Championship qualifier

Photo: Richard Webb via Wikimedia Commons (CC-BY-SA 2.0)

Course 4
Leven Links
📍 Leven, Fife

Claims the title of first course in the world with 18 tees and 18 separate greens. A six-time Open Championship final qualifying venue with rolling fairways, sand dunes, fast greens, burns, and pot bunkers. The 18th hole — a 458-yard par 4 — is considered the toughest closing hole in all of Scotland.

🏆 6x Open Championship qualifying venue
🔥 18th hole: 458 yds — toughest finisher in Scotland
📅 Golf history dating to the mid-1800s

Photo: Mike Pennington via Wikimedia Commons (CC-BY-SA 2.0)

The Old Course at St Andrews
📍 St. Andrews, Fife — Operated by St. Andrews Links Trust

The birthplace of golf. The most famous course in the world. 600+ years of history on every hole. Swilcan Bridge. Hell Bunker. The Road Hole. The Valley of Sin. Par 72, 7,305 yards. The Old Course ballot lottery is still in play — if it comes through, everything else on the itinerary pales in comparison. And even if it doesn’t, you’ll walk every inch of it anyway.

📅 Golf played here since the early 1400s • Par 72 • 7,305 yards
🏆 30 Open Championships hosted — more than any other venue
🏞 7 double greens • Swilcan Bridge (700 years old) • Hell Bunker covers 300 sq yards

Photo: Bahnfrend via Wikimedia Commons (CC-BY-SA 4.0)
